First Aid Instructions

Essential medical procedures with step-by-step visual guides

Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)

Difficulty:
1

Check Responsiveness

Tap shoulders and shout "Are you okay?" Check for breathing and pulse.

2

Call 911

If no response, immediately call emergency services or have someone else call.

3

Position Hands

Place heel of one hand on center of chest, other hand on top. Interlock fingers.

4

Compress Chest

Push hard and fast at 100-120 compressions per minute. Allow chest to fully recoil.

5

Give Rescue Breaths

After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths. Tilt head, lift chin, pinch nose.

Important Tips

  • Continue CPR until help arrives or person starts breathing
  • Use "Stayin' Alive" beat for compression rhythm
  • Don't stop unless you're too exhausted to continue

Bleeding Control

Difficulty:
1

Apply Direct Pressure

Use clean cloth or gauze. Press firmly on the wound with your hand.

2

Elevate the Injury

Raise the injured area above heart level to reduce blood flow.

3

Use Pressure Points

Apply pressure to major arteries if direct pressure isn't working.
